Introduction to the Revenue Model

Your trust is what allows us to save lives. That's why we want you to know exactly how we generate and manage the revenue that powers this project and, most importantly, how those funds directly impact animal rescue.

Pawthways operates on a simple, transparent two-rail system: Commercial Revenue (sales of digital products and future upsells) and a fixed Charitable Allocation (40% of revenue is donated to verified animal welfare causes). We keep these rails separate so every user can see where money comes from and where it goes.

This page explains the flow, the exact distribution, and how we report it publicly.

Sources of Income

Pawthways generates revenue primarily through the sale of digital products and educational resources. Every product includes materials that empower users to create similar content and business models.

  • Product Sales: artbooks, AI packs, music and creation resources.
  • Upsells & Merchandising: higher-tier content, future digital collectibles or merchandise.
  • Indirect Donations: 40% of revenue is allocated to charitable causes — this acts as the donations channel.
How income is generated

Sales are processed through our platform (Whop). Each purchase funds both project growth and charitable allocations. Every product includes behind-the-scenes material so the community can learn and reproduce our process.

Transparency in Distribution

We publish transaction-level aggregates on the Transparency page and provide monthly reports. No personal data of donors is published; only aggregated numbers and proof documents (receipts, invoices) linked via secure URLs.

Technical implementation & data

  1. We use the existing public endpoint: /api/admin-stats. It returns aggregated numbers used by the chart (see below).
  2. The chart attempts to fetch real data; if unavailable it falls back to a 40% / 60% default.
  3. Public views only expose aggregated values and proof documents (no PII).
NOTE: allocate and audit_donations_log already run in the backend; this page only reads aggregates to show public transparency. For integration with NGOs see the webhook spec in our docs.
